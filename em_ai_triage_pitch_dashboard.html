Overview

Emergency department triage accuracy is critical — undertriage of high-acuity patients carries significant patient safety risk. Large language models (LLMs) such as GPT-4 and Claude have recently demonstrated an ability to interpret clinical text, yet their performance on structured triage tasks remains poorly characterised. This study proposes a systematic evaluation of LLM-assigned Emergency Severity Index (ESI) scores against those of board-certified emergency physicians using a standardised set of clinical vignettes, with analysis of accuracy, undertriage rate, and performance variation across chief complaint categories and patient demographics.

Why This Research Matters Now

Research Questions
Proposed Questions — Select One Primary Focus
Primary Recommendation

"How accurately do large language models (GPT-4, Claude) assign Emergency Severity Index triage scores compared to emergency physicians using standardised clinical vignettes — and in which chief complaint categories do LLMs most frequently undertriage?"

Alternative A · Equity Focus

"Do large language models exhibit demographic bias in emergency triage scoring when identical clinical presentations are assigned different patient ages, sexes, or races?"

Alternative B · Prompt Engineering

"Does providing additional clinical context — vitals, past medical history, medications — to an LLM improve ESI triage scoring accuracy compared to chief complaint alone?"

Gaps in Current Literature
Existing studies are small and single-center — rigorous multi-LLM replication with pre-registered methods is absent
No study has systematically identified which chief complaint categories carry the highest undertriage risk with LLM assistance
No published equity analysis — whether LLMs assign different triage scores based on patient demographics for identical presentations is unknown
Head-to-head comparison of multiple current LLMs (GPT-4, Claude, Gemini) on the same standardised cases has not been performed
The effect of prompt design and clinical context quantity on triage accuracy is unstudied
Literature is concentrated in urban academic EDs — community and rural ED contexts remain unrepresented
